# NammaASHA

## Roles and Responsibilities of ASHA Workers

### Her Core Roles
- First point of contact for health needs of women and children
- Bridge between the village and the public health system (PHC, ANM, Anganwadi)

### What She Does Daily
- Counsels women on pregnancy, safe delivery, breastfeeding, immunization, contraception
- Escorts/refers pregnant women and sick children to health facilities
- Distributes basic medicines — ORS, IFA tablets, contraceptives, delivery kits
- Creates awareness on nutrition, sanitation, hygiene, and healthy practices

### Documentation & Reporting
- Tracks pregnancies, births, deaths, immunizations in her village
- Reports disease outbreaks or unusual health events to the PHC

---

## Main Grievances

1. Expected to work 2-3 hours a day on paper — reality is 5-8 hours daily
2. Over 30 tasks assigned, many not even health-related
3. Maintains 10-20 physical registers manually
4. Same data entered multiple times in different formats
5. No one to consult when she encounters an unusual case in the field

---

## What Our Website Is Solving

1. **Language Barrier** — Our website provides a fully local language interface in Kannada, allowing ASHA workers to interact with the tool comfortably without any English.

2. **No Decision Support** — Our website provides an AI model trained on official HBNC and NHM guidelines that she can consult in the field when she has doubts or encounters unusual cases.

3. **Paper-Based Documentation** — Our website digitizes all her registers and records, eliminating physical paperwork entirely and making data accessible and storable on her phone.

4. **Redundant Data Entry** — Our website captures patient data once through a single entry form and automatically maps it to all required government formats, eliminating the need to re-enter the same information multiple times.
